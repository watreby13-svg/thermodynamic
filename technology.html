Heat transfer, simplified

Three ways heat moves — and how we stop each one.

C

Conduction

Heat travelling through solid materials. Our thermal barriers are made of low-conductivity materials that resist heat flow between compartments.

↗

Convection

Heat moving through air or liquid. Each compartment is sealed independently, preventing air circulation that would carry heat between zones.

~

Radiation

Infrared heat transfer between surfaces. Reflective barriers inside the insulation layer bounce radiant heat back toward its source.

The proof

Why mince pie stays hot while ice cream stays cold

The internal thermal barriers create independent temperature zones. Heat from the hot compartment cannot cross into the cold compartment because:

  • B
    Physical separation — Each compartment has its own insulated wall
  • X
    No air bridge — Sealed gaskets prevent convection between compartments
  • R
    Reflective barrier — Inner foil layer reflects radiant heat
  • M
    Mass effect — Each compartment's contents act as their own thermal mass
